The High-Low Method

The high-low method is one of the methods we could use to determine the cost formula for a certain mixed cost. It is determined using the highest and lowest level of activity.

For example, we can use the high low method to determine the cost formula for the following data:

Hours Total Cost
625 7,950
500 7,400
800 9,800

The variable cost for this data is then:

Change in cost / Change in hours =
(9,800 - 7,400) / (800 - 500) = 2,400 / 300 = $8/hour

The total fixed cost is calculated by finding the difference between the total cost and total variable cost. We can use any of the data points for this.

Here we use the data point for 800 hours to find total fixed cost:

Fixed cost = Total cost - total variable cost =
9,800 - 8(800) = $3,400

Least-Squares Regression

There are more methods to calculate the cost formula. This method uses computer software to fit a straight line against a set of data. This method may sometimes provide a more accurate representation of the cost formula of a company.

Contribution Format

The contribution format is an alternative method to reporting income statements.

In the contribution format, costs are organized by variable and fixed instead of by period and product. So whereas a traditional income statement may look like this:

Sales
Less Cost of Goods Sold
Gross Margin
Less Operating Expenses
Net Operating Income
$ 100,000
70,000
$ 30,000
20,000
$ 10,000

A contribution format income statement may look like this (with the differences bolded):

Sales
Less Variable Expenses
Contribution Margin
Less Fixed Expenses
Net Operating Income

60,000
$ 40,000
