Contribution Margin

Say we are given the following income statement:

For The Month Ending June 2017

Less Variable Expenses
Contribution Margin
Less Fixed Expenses
Net Operating Income
$ 250,000
150,000

80,000
$ 20,000

The per-unit contribution margin would just be the contribution margin divided by the total number of units:

UCM = 100,000 / 500 = $200

The CM ratio is the contribution margin divided by total sales:

CM Ratio = 100,000 / 250,000 = %40

The CM ratio can also be defined as the increase in contribution margin for every dollar increase in sales. For this bicycle company, every dollar increase in sales will increase contribution margin by $0.4.

Example Problem 1

The bicycle company believes it can increase sales to 540 units if it spends $10,000 on advertising. Would you recommend that the advertising campaign be undertaken?

Solution

The increase in contribution margin will be:

UCM x Change in Sales = $200 x (540 - 500) = +$8,000

However, fixed expenses will increase by $10,000. Therefore the change in net income will be:

+8,000 + (-10,000) = -$2,000

Therefore it is not recommended that the advertising campaign be undertaken.

Example Problem 2

What is the profit impact if the company cuts its selling price by $20 and increases its advertising budget by $15,000, increasing sales from 500 to 650 units per month?

Solution

The company's original selling price is:

Sales / # Units = $250,000 / 500 = $500/unit

Therefore the new total sales would be:

Sales price x # Units = (500 - 20) x 650 = $312,000

The new contribution margin would be:

CM ratio x Sales = %40 x 312,000 = $124,800

Fixed expenses increased by $15,000, and so the new net income would be:

124,800 - (80,000 + 15,000) = $29,800

And so the effect on net income would be an increase by $9,800.

Break-Even Analysis

The Equation Method

There are two equations that we can use to find the break-even point, depending on whether we want to find it in terms of sales quantity or in terms of sales dollars.

In terms of sales quantity:

Break-Even Q =
Total Fixed Expenses / (Unit Selling Price - Unit Variable Cost)

In terms of sales dollars:

Break-Even Sales $ =
Total Fixed Expenses / (1 - Variable Cost (as a % of sales))

The Contribution Method

We can refactor the above equations in terms of contribution margin.

Since (Unit Selling Price - Unit Variable Cost) is equal to Unit Contribution Margin, the equation can be rewritten as:

Break-Even Q =
Total Fixed Expenses / Unit Contribution Margin

Similarly, 1 - Variable Cost (as a % of sales) is equal to the CM ratio. Therefore:

Break-Even Sales $ =
Total Fixed Expenses / CM Ratio

Target Profit Analysis

The equation and contribution margin methods may be used to determine the sales volume needed to achieve a particular target profit.

To do this, we just add the target profit to the fixed expenses as follows:

Break-Even Sales $ =
Total Fixed Expenses + Target Profit / CM Ratio

There may be questions that ask for after-tax or before-tax target profit. In this case, we have to convert to before-tax target profit before doing any calculations.

The formula for this is:

Before-Tax Target Profit =
After-Tax Target Profit / (1 - Tax Rate)

Example Problem

The break-even point in sales for XYZ Company is $360,000, and the company's contribution margin ratio is 20%. Its income tax rate is 40%.

If XYZ desires an after-tax profit of $84,000, what would total sales have to be?

Show Solution

An $84,000 after-tax profit and an income tax rate of 40% translates to a before-tax profit of:

84,000/(1-0.40) = $140,000

We then need to find the total fixed expenses of the company. Break-even sales is $360,000, therefore:

360,000 = (Fixed Expenses)/0.20
Fixed Expenses = 360,000 x 0.20 = $72,000

We then put fixed expenses and the target profit together to find the required sales amount:

Required sales $ = (72,000 + 140,000)/0.20 = $1,060,000

Margin of Safety

The margin of safety is the excess of budgeted (or actual) sales over the break-even volume of sales.

Margin of Safety ($) = Total sales - Break-even sales
Margin of Safety (%) = Margin of Safety ($) / Total sales
Margin of Safety (#) = #Units sold - #BE units = Margin of Safety ($) / Unit Selling Price

Operating Leverage

The degree of operating leverage is a measure of how sensitive net income is to a % change in sales.

Degree of operating leverage = Contribution Margin / Net Income

Essentially it tells you what % income would change given a % change in sales.

Sales Mix

Often a company would not be selling only one product. The sales mix of a company is the relative proportion in which a company's products are sold.

The overall CM ratio of a company is equal to the weighted average of the individual ratios.

The overall contribution margin ratio is the weighted average of the individual contribution margins. To find the weighted average, we first need to find the sales mix:

Total sales = 250,000 + 300,000 = 550,000
Bikes = 250,000/550,000 = 45%
Carts = 300,000/550,000 = 55%

The overall CM ratio is therefore:

Overall CM ratio = 0.45(40) + 0.55(55) = 48.25
