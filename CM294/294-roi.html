Return on Investment and Residual Income

Return on Investment

Return on investment is defined as the ratio between income and invested capital:

ROI = Income / Invested Capital

Income and invested capital is quite ambiguous, and so:

  • Income is generally operating income.
  • Invested Capital is generally operating assets; but average operating assets is preferred. That is:

1/2 x (Beginning Assets + Ending Assets)

The return on investment is used to evaluate individual investments, profit-centers, or investment-centers.

ROI can also be rewritten in terms of sales margin and capital turnover:

ROI = Sales Margin x Capital Turnover

  • Sales Margin is the % of sales dollars remaining as profit after expenses are covered.
  • Capital Turnover is the # of sales dollars generated by every dollar of invested capital.

There are three ways to increase ROI:

  1. Reduce operating expenses - Improves sales margin
  2. Increase sales - Increases capital turnover
  3. Reduce invested capital (operating assets) - Increases capital turnover

Residual Income

Residual income is another method to measure performance. Residual income is defined as the operating income above some minimum return on invested capital. Unlike ROI, residual income is a dollar figure.

Generally, the proposal to invest should be accepted if RI > 0.

The formula to find residual income is:

RI = Net income - (Required rate of return)(Avg. operating assets)

Example Problem 1

The Wyalusing Company is a manufacturer of prefabricated houses. One of its divisions, the Fairmont division, is a designated investment centre. It uses ROI as a performance measure with investment defined as average productive assets.

All investments are expected to earn a minimum return of 15%.

Fairmont had an investment opportunity with an estimated ROI of 18%. The income statement (in 000s) for the Fairmont division is as follows. The division's productive assets were $12,600,000 at the end of the year, a 5% increase over the beginning of the year.

Calculate ROI and RI

Total average assets is equal to:

(12,600,000 + 12,600,000/1.05) / 2 = 12,300,000

Therefore ROI is:

ROI = Net Income / Avg. Assets = 2,460,000 / 12,300,000 = 20%

And RI is:

RI = Net Income - (Required rate of return x avg. assets) =
2,460,000 - (15% x 12,300,000) = $615,000

Would Fairmont likely have accepted the investment opportunity if RI was used instead of ROI?

Yes, because at the current net income and at the current required rate of return, RI is $615,000. Since this is > 0, the investment would have been accepted.
