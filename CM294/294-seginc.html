Segmented Income Statements

A segmented income statement uses the contribution format, and separates 'traceable' fixed costs from 'common' fixed costs.

  • Traceable fixed costs would be eliminated if the segment does not exist.
  • Common fixed costs would remain even if the segment does not exist.

It is possible that a traceable fixed cost of one segment is a common fixed cost of another segment.

Segmented income statements allows for the calculation of a segment margin. Common fixed costs are not relevant to the segment margin.

Segment margin =
Contribution margin - traceable fixed costs of segment
