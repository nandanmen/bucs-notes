Standard Costing

Standard Costing System

A standard costing system allows us to monitor, analyze, and amend operational strategies on an ongoing basis.

A standard is a benchmark for measuring performance. In this course there are two types:

  • Cost standards
  • Quantity standards

These standards are compared with actual costs to find variances. These variances are used to measure and investigate performance.

Direct Materials

There are two standards for direct materials:

Standard material price, which is the landed cost of needed materials for one unit.

  • A landed cost is the total price of a product once it has arrived at a buyer's door. This includes the original price, all transportation fees, customs, duties, taxes, insurance, currency conversion, crafting, handling and payment fees.

Standard material quantity, which is the material needed to produce one unit of product.

  • Allows for unavoidable and/or reasonable waste, spoilage, rejects. Based on a bill of materials.

Variances

The direct materials price variance or MVP (Materials Price Variance), is calculated as follows. This is based on amount purchased.

Actual quantity x (Actual price - Standard price)

The direct materials quantity variance or MQP (Materials Quantity Variance), is calculated as follows. This is based on amount used.

Standard price x (Actual quantity - Standard quantity)

Direct Labour

Standard labour rate is the estimated hourly cost of total compensation.

  • Includes fringe benefits and taxes.

Standard labour quantity is the engineered time per unit, i.e. usual number of hours needed to manufacture the unit.

  • Allows for machine downtime, idle time, breaks, etc.

Variances

Labour rate variance (LRV) is calculated as follows:

Actual hours x (Actual rate - Standard rate)

Labour efficiency variance (LEV) is calculated as follows:

Standard rate x (Actual hours - Standard hours)

Variable MOH

Standard overhead rate is the variable part of the pre-determined overhead rate.

Standard overhead quantity is the typical amount of activity that is used to calculate the overhead rate.

Variances

Variable overhead spending variance (VOSV) is calculated as follows:

Actual hours x (Actual rate - Standard rate)

Variable overhead efficiency variance (VOEV) is calculated as follows:

Standard rate x (Actual hours - Standard hours)
